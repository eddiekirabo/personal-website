Walmart Sales Data Analysis

This project focuses on analyzing Walmart sales data to identify the top-performing branches and products, understand sales trends for different products, and study customer behavior. The primary goal is to explore how sales strategies can be optimized and improved. The dataset was sourced from the Kaggle Walmart Sales Forecasting Competition.
The competition involved providing historical sales data from 45 Walmart stores across various regions.

Project Purpose

The main objective of this project is to gain insights into Walmart's sales data to better understand the factors influencing sales performance across different branches.

See SQL Script

About the Data

The dataset was obtained from the Kaggle Walmart Sales Forecasting Competition and contains sales records from three Walmart branches located in Mandalay, Yangon, and Naypyitaw. It consists of 17 columns and 1,000 rows of data.

Analysis Overview

Product Analysis

Analyze the performance of different product lines, identify the best-performing lines, and highlight product lines that require improvement.

Sales Analysis

Study sales trends across product lines to evaluate the effectiveness of sales strategies and determine necessary adjustments to increase sales.

Customer Analysis

Examine customer segments, their purchasing patterns, and the profitability of each segment.

Approach

Data Wrangling

Inspect the data for NULL and missing values, and replace or handle them as necessary.
Create a database to organize the data and remove any null values by setting fields as NOT NULL.

Feature Engineering

Add a time_of_day column to categorize sales into Morning, Afternoon, and Evening periods to determine when most sales occur.
Add a day_name column to track which day of the week transactions occur, helping to identify the busiest days.
Add a month_name column to understand the sales and profit patterns across months.

    • Revenue and Profit Calculations

    COGS (Cost of Goods Sold) = unit_price * quantity
    VAT = 5% * COGS
    Total Sales = COGS + VAT
    Gross Profit = Total Sales - COGS
    Gross Margin = Gross Profit / Total Sales

    Example Calculation

    Unit Price = 45.79
    Quantity = 7
    COGS = 45.79 * 7 = 320.53
    VAT = 5% of 320.53 = 16.03
    Total Sales = 320.53 + 16.03 = 336.56
    Gross Margin = (Gross Income / Total Sales) = 4.76%

                                    WHEN 'time' BETWEEN "00:00:00" and "12:00:00" THEN "Morning"
                                    WHEN 'time' BETWEEN "12:01:00" and "16:00:00" THEN "Morning"
                                    ELSE "Evening"
                                END
                                ) AS time_of_day
                                FROM sales;

                                    ELSE "Evening"
